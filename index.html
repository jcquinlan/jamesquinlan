about me

I've been learning web design and development for the last few years after being inspired by experiences with Processing and Java. I wanted to write code but I also needed a suitable medium through which to share what I've written. Since learning to design and develop the web on my time and through classes, I've taken numerous jobs as a freelancer, worked at a small design studio creating responsive sites for a variety of clients, designed an Android app, and have even begun learning application frameworks like Meteor and AngularJS. I pride myself in being an autodidact, and finding time to learn more about web development while maintaining a healthy art practice.

What I look forward to most in my design career is working with a small, dedicated team of designers and developers to tackle a diverse range of design challenges, ranging from websites to apps, and beyond. I find that I work excellently in a team environment, and with a small team I am afforded more time to ensure things like cross-browser compatability, effecient load times, and superb accessibility.

my work

First Steps Physical Therapy

I built this website for a local physical therapist that focuses on treating children. I wanted to keep a light and playful atmosphere in order to accentuate the caring and safe nature of the office, but I also needed to cater to the professional side of the practice. The site is fully responsive, as I used a custom export of bootstrap for the grid system. I also took all the photographs used on the site.

LLB Point-of-Purchase Displays

I built this fixed-width site while working at Mind Your Design, a design firm in my hometown of Doylestown. The client requested an unresponsive website, so I worked using simple HTML and CSS to render the mockups I was provided by my supervisor. An independent developer was hired to create the custom Javascript used in various parts of the website.

McBrick Building Group

The McBrick site is another example of me having to build a site off of mockups provided to me by Mind Your Design. This time, however, the site was responsive. I experimented with numerous grid systems, including Skeleton, Foundation, and 960gs, but ultimately decided to use a custom export of Bootstrap containing the absolute minimum needed to build the site.

View Project

Murmur

Murmur is an Android app for which I am lead designer. The app is scheduled for a v1.0 release in late 2015 and is a simple experiment into flocking mechanisms. I took the name from the phenomonon of murmuration, in which birds create undulating, fluid groups as a defense mechanism to ward off predators. I knew I wanted to experiment with flocking as a mechanism to create a more relatable or organic dynamic between the player and the "character".
